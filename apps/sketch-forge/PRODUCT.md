# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Engineering students and software developers who need to build, revisit, and explain technical understanding over time. They are working with system diagrams, algorithms, architecture notes, lecture material, debugging traces, and reference material that is too visual or connected to live comfortably in a plain text document.

## Product Purpose

Sketch Forge is a long-term visual knowledge base for technical work. It helps people organize durable engineering notes and references on an infinite canvas, then use those notes to explain diagrams and concepts to other people. Success means a user can return to an old idea, understand its structure quickly, extend it without starting over, and share the explanation when needed.

## Positioning

Sketch Forge is a visual-first technical notebook where engineering knowledge stays connected, spatial, and explainable instead of becoming a pile of disconnected documents.

## Brand Personality

Focused, curious, and precise. The product should feel like a calm workspace for serious thinking: approachable enough for learning, capable enough for real engineering work, and expressive enough to preserve how people actually reason. The public landing page may be more distinctive and narrative, while the canvas and dashboard should remain quiet, trustworthy, and task-oriented.

## Anti-references

Avoid the shape of a generic all-purpose productivity suite, a sterile enterprise administration dashboard, or a toy drawing app that treats sketching as decoration. Avoid gamified learning patterns, excessive visual noise, gratuitous novelty in standard controls, and interfaces that make technical content compete with the chrome around it.

## Design Principles

- Make visual structure the primary way technical ideas are captured and understood.
- Preserve continuity: notes should become more useful as they accumulate, not harder to find.
- Keep the tool quiet so the user’s thinking remains the most prominent thing on screen.
- Support explanation as a first-class outcome, not an afterthought to private note-taking.
- Favor familiar, reliable interaction patterns, with personality expressed through the canvas and the brand surface.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for the web experience. Maintain readable contrast, keyboard access for core navigation and canvas actions, visible focus states, non-color-only status communication, useful labels for icon controls, and reduced-motion behavior. Technical notes and diagrams should have accessible text equivalents wherever the interaction supports them.
